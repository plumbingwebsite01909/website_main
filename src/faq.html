Common Plumbing Issues

Low water pressure can result from mineral buildup in aerators, leaks in the supply line, issues with the water main, or problems with the water heater. Our plumbers can diagnose the cause and fix it quickly.

Signs include unusually high water bills, wet spots on walls or floors, mold or mildew, or hearing running water when all fixtures are off. We use thermal imaging and advanced leak detection to find hidden leaks without damaging your walls.

Don't use drain cleaners — they can damage pipes. Instead, try a plunger or snake for minor clogs. For persistent problems, call us for professional hydro-jetting or camera inspection to identify the blockage.

Discolored water is usually rust or sediment from old pipes or mineral buildup. It's typically harmless but indicates your pipes may need attention. Let our team assess whether replacement or flushing is needed.

Maintenance & Prevention

We recommend a professional plumbing inspection every 1–2 years, especially for homes over 30 years old. Annual inspections are ideal if you have aging pipes or recurring issues.

Use drain screens, avoid pouring grease down drains, don't flush non-biodegradable items, and run hot water regularly. Annual professional drain cleaning and maintenance helps prevent serious clogs.

Most water heaters last 10–15 years. Signs of failure include no hot water, strange noises, leaks, or rust-colored water. We can assess your heater and recommend repair or replacement.
